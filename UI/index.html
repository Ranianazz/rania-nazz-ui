Interaction Design Comparison: Orb.Farm vs Holdspace

Introduction

This page analyses and contrasts the interaction design of two experimental web-based experiences: Orb.Farm by Max Bittker and Holdspace by Carlos Yusim. Both projects invite users into interactive, audiovisual environments, but they take very different approaches to interaction design.

Information Design

Orb.Farm presents a simulation of a living fishbowl with no instructions upfront. Users discover its elements — water, fish, grass — by interacting freely. The lack of instructions encourages exploration, but it may confuse first-time users.

Holdspace is minimal: a black screen with subtle visual effects. The only clue is the prompt to press the spacebar. As users press or hold space, audio plays and a loading bar at the top indicates progress. It’s abstract, but intentionally so, guiding users via sound and movement.

Comparison: Orb.Farm hides information to reward experimentation. Holdspace simplifies it to one clear interaction with focused sensory feedback. Orb is more sandbox; Holdspace is more guided.

Mapping

Orb.Farm uses standard point-and-click mapping — drag, drop, and place elements with the mouse. Some tools aren't immediately obvious, but over time, the interface feels natural for sandbox play.

Holdspace relies on a single key: the spacebar. Tap it, and individual sounds play. Hold it, and the full track unfolds. The mapping is direct, intuitive, and tightly tied to the experience’s concept.

Comparison: Orb.Farm requires trial-and-error to understand control mappings. Holdspace’s single-key input makes the experience feel immediate and accessible.

Characterisation

Orb.Farm feels like a digital terrarium — peaceful, experimental, and slightly retro. It invites calm curiosity. The visuals are pixelated and charming, with a DIY, open-ended character.

Holdspace feels like an art piece. Its aesthetic is sleek and moody. It leans into minimalism, mystery, and atmosphere. The character is reflective, almost meditative.

Comparison: Orb.Farm encourages active, creative tinkering. Holdspace creates an emotional, ambient mood with minimal input. One is playful and systemic; the other is poetic and sensory.

Feedback

Orb.Farm gives visual feedback — objects animate, grow, or change over time. However, there's little immediate response to user actions. Changes are slow, mirroring natural processes.

Holdspace gives instant audio and visual feedback. Tapping space gives sound and animation. Holding space triggers a loading bar, building anticipation. It’s fast, clear, and emotionally engaging.

Comparison: Orb.Farm uses subtle, delayed feedback. Holdspace responds immediately. This reflects their different goals: simulation versus sensory experience.
