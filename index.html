Interaction Design Comparison: Orb.Farm vs Holdspace

Introduction

This page will display an analysis of two experimental interactive web-based experiences: Orb.Farm by Max Bittker and Holdspace by Carlos Yusim. Both of these projects Invite users into an interactive, audiovisual environment, however They take different approaches to their interaction design.

Information Design

Orb.Farm displays a simulation of a fishbowl with no obvious instructions upfront. The users discover elements such as: water, fish, and grass by interacting with it freely. The lack of instructions encourages exploration, but it at the same time it may confuse first-time users.

Holdspace is a minimal interactive web-based experience. A black screen with subtle visual effects. The only clue is the prompt to press the spacebar. As users press or hold space, audio plays, and a loading bar at the top indicates progress. The 3d The image is intentionally abstract, guiding users via sound and movement.

Comparison: Orb.Farm hides a lot of information to encourage experimentation. While Holdspace simplifies it to one clear interaction with a focus on sensory feedback. Orb has no guide; Holdspace has a guide on how to use it.

Mapping

Orb.Farm utilises standard point and click mapping with a drag and drop tool to place elements with the mouse. Some tools aren't obvious at first, but over time, the interface feels clear.

Holdspace depends on a single button, the spacebar. When you tap it, individual sounds play. Hold it, and the full track plays. The mapping is direct, intuitive, and tightly tied to the experience’s concept.

Comparison: Orb.Farm requires a lot of trial and error to understand the control mappings. Meanwhile, Holdspace’s single-key input makes the experience feel immediate and accessible.

Characterisation

Orb.Farm, this retro experience feels like a digital fishbowl with its peaceful, experimental element that invites calm curiosity. The visuals are pixelated and charming, with a DIY, open-ended character.

Holdspace gives a sense of an art piece. With its aesthetic that is a sleek and moody. It leans into minimalism, mystery, and atmosphere. The character is reflective, almost meditative.

Comparison: Orb.Farm encourages active and creative play. However, Holdspace creates an emotional, ambient mood with minimal input. One is playful and structural; the other is ambient and sensory.

Feedback

Orb.Farm gives visual feedback on the objects animation, and Changes over time. However, there isn't much immediate response to the users actions. Changes are slow, making it look like natural processes.

Holdspace gives instant audio and visual feedback. When Tapping space it gives sound and animation. When holding space it activates the loading bar, building anticipation. It’s fast, clear, and emotionally engaging.

Comparison: Orb.Farm uses subtle, delayed feedback. Meanwhile Holdspace responds immediately. This reflects their different goals: simulation versus sensory experience.
